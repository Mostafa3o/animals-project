American Alligator

  • Common Name: Bald Eagle
  • Scientific Name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus
  • Habitat: Near large bodies of open water with abundant food supply and old-growth trees for nesting.
  • Diet: Carnivore; primarily feeds on fish, but will also eat small mammals and carrion.
  • Wingspan: 6 to 7.5 feet (1.8 to 2.3 meters).
  • Lifespan: 20 to 30 years in the wild.
  • Behavior: Known for its impressive flight and keen eyesight; a symbol of strength and freedom in the United States.
